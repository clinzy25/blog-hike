Goat Rocks Traverse

August 2025 | 3 days | 38.75 miles | +11490' | 

Caltopo Link

Two years straight I have been smoked out of hiking around Mount Rainier in September. Not this year.

For this trip I tried making my own granola breakfasts as I have been paying almost $10 each for the MREs, and the recipe is very basic. If you are interested:

  • Sturdy MRE bags like this. These are actually reusable and dishwasher safe.
  • 1 Cup any kind of granola
  • 1/4 cup of dry milk
  • Dehydrated fruit. Trader Joe's has a good selection of these.
  • Add cold water and stir when ready to eat.

The day before the hike I finished work around 2 PM, picked up a rental car and headed to Berry Patch Trailhead. If you are coming from the north, there is a 45 min long dirt road off of US12 with tons of rivets that unavoidably shake the shit out of your car. I arrived in the evening, ate some leftover pasta and crashed (slept) in my car.

I started the next morning at 6:30AM with a swift ascent up to Goat Ridge Lookout. Barely 2 miles into this hike you start to get some awesome views.

Mount Adams from Goat Ridge Lookout

First look at the main ridge

Marmots everywhere out here.

Shallowly ascending Goat Ridge to an unnamed pass where the Lily Basin trail begins.

From the pass you can view a majority of the traverse through Lily Basin beneath the Goat Rocks themselves. The weather was 10/10 on this day I don't think it went above 75 degress.

I ditched my pack here, grabbed a water bottle and headed up to Hawkeye Point.

Hawkeye Point from below. There were actually lots hawks circling the area. Go figure.

Mt Rainier finally showed up. Late as usual.

Looking back at a tangential unnamed ridge with stupendous rock formations. I traversed this ridge shortly after.

At Hawkeye Point, Goat Lake comes into view. There were bowls of nails and iron rods sticking out of the rocks here, maybe remnants of an old structure. I met a man who gave me a gummy bear.

This was also one of the many points where you could see 3 stratavolcanos.

I wanted to scramble the ridge around the lake but there was a sheer cliff after Hawkeye that you would probably want to rappel down.

This ridge in the opposite direction was much more accessible, headed there next. Still only packing a water bottle here so I did some trailrunning.

Unnamed ridge scramble 001. Nothing crazy.

Except for the Marmots...

Heading back down to the pass to grab my pack.

At this point I had completed most of my itenerary for the first day. I don't think it was even noon yet. I had seen tons of campsites already so I decided to throw out my itenerary and do whatever, at no particular pace. I wasn't even sure if I was going to complete the entire loop, since the last half of my intended route was mostly through low lying forest, which just wasn't as interesting as the core region. I just had a few of goals in mind:

  • Knife Edge PCT section
  • Old Snowy Mountain
  • Goat Rocks main ridge scramble
  • Snowgrass Flats

Shortly after this I decided to set up camp either around Goat Lake pending crowds or a bit further in Lily Basin. Then if I had enough time I would hike up to the ridge and trail run the Knife Edge, come back down and end the day.

So now heading down into the basin to set up camp.

These rock formations were otherworldly.

There were tons of flowers in this section. I think this was past peak bloom but I'm definitely going to visit next year in early July, probably for a day hike.

A Ptarmigan in a tree. These birds have the strangest call.

Goat Lake. There were lots of people here, many day hikers and backpakers had already set up camp. I decided to find a more secluded spot further down.

Turning around from Goat Lake to the expanse of Lily Basin.

Found a nice wooded campsite and looked back at the traverse. Hawkeye point and the basin containing Goat Lake are in the image below.

At this point I set up camp, ate lunch, and took a nap. The rest of the day I had a very light pack with just some water, snacks and some safety essentials. I started up the ridge toward Old Snowy Mountain around 2pm to check out the Knife Edge PCT section. From my campsite I went back toward Goat Lake maybe a quarter mile and turned right onto a trail that ascended sharply to the east. This trail for some reason was not on my paper map but was visible on Caltopo.

The vegetation quickly faded and gave way to boulders and talus. There was some trivial route finding up to the ridge.

The knife edge from below.

Old Snowy below. I climb this the next day.

Meeting up with the PCT here. I think this was my first time on the PCT. I started to see a steady stream of weathered thru hikers moving north. I dropped my pack here and continued with just a water bottle.

This trail is so badass. Panoramic views and almost sheer cliffs on both sides.

I scrambled one of the peaks on this section, but it was absolute crap choss and I got stuck in a class 4 section on the way down. Do not recommend. At least got this sick shot with some bird action.

So ends day 1, with some mid-ass Teriyaki MRE that I probably could have eaten two of. Absolute banger of a day though. Sometimes backpacking trips can be a bit dull when you have to do longer distances and have your pack on all day. It was very refreshing to just hang out in one area and do a bunch of different activites. I was completely exhausted though, clocking 15 miles and 6000 feet of vert, with some trail running. Slept like a baby.
